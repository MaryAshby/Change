2) What are the pro's and cons of 3 visualizations techniques discussed in chapter 8?

Features of a Time Series

A time series is a visualization that shows change over equally spaced time intervals on the x-axis and the variables on the y-axis (Cairo, p. 201).
  • Trend - how do the variables change in time?
  • Seasonality - are there consistent and periodic fluctuations?
  • Noise - are there random changes?
The three types of visualizations Cairo writes about in Chapter eight are: indexes, ratios, and logs. These various time based series help to tease out the trends and seasonal changes, and smooth out noise.

Features of an Index

Indexing a time based chart will show the percentage of change over time. It is tied to a single index value or the average of several values (Cairo, p. 214). It is useful for representing relative change. It is limited by its tether to one value.

Features of Ratio Charts

A ratio time series allows comparison of the "rate of change of each time period in comparison to the previous one" (p. 214). It can show changes that might get smoothed over in other time based charts, however, it is not good for for examining longer trends in the data, as it the chage rate is based on the new period divided by the old.

Features of a Logarithmic Scale

When looking at data that increases exponentially, the other time series charts may not visually represent the series well. Using a log base, the data points are set by their magnitude in relation to the previous point. It would not be useful in instances with small increments of change.
